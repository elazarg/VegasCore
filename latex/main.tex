\begin{abstract}
A VegasCore program denotes a finite game of incomplete information.  The
surface is a straight-line calculus whose control flow is value-independent:
four constructs draw public randomness from nature, let a player commit a
sealed value constrained by a guard, reveal a sealed value to everyone, and
return per-player payoffs over the public state.  Every bound value is public
or sealed to exactly one player, and reveal is the sole sealed-to-public
channel, so a single program declares an information structure, a strategic
structure, and payoffs at once; read as a probabilistic program, its latent
draws may be made by strategic players rather than only by chance.

Our contribution is a family of \emph{faithfulness relations}, mechanized in
Lean, that together carry a game-theoretic fact about the denoted game through
compilation, scheduling, presentation, and descent toward a concrete runtime.
Each relation has a distinct algebraic shape, and the shape fixes what a result
may forget as it crosses that boundary.

An \emph{adequacy} recovers the source-free event graph as the program's
denotation: at terminal reachable states, outcomes are exactly the source
payoffs.  Three \emph{equivalences} let a result move without loss: reordering
independent events leaves the outcome unchanged; behavioral and mixed-pure
strategy carriers match one-player deviations, so solution concepts transport
and not payoffs alone; and a presentation equivalence relates the native game to
standard imperfect-information game-forms (factored-observation stochastic games
and extensive-form games).  A \emph{refinement order} shows that an executable
backend preserves the utility distribution and---under bounded trace
utilities---pulls equilibria back, so a compatible backend adds no spurious
equilibria.  The visibility-aware event graph beneath these results is the
load-bearing mechanism, not the contribution itself.

The development is fully mechanized in Lean: the surface is
a kernel calculus, strategies are carried by the compiled frontier game, and
the runtime descent is proved sound and exercised on abstract backends.  The
construction is shaped by a concrete
setting---Ethereum/EVM and similar public,
adversarial runtimes, whose visible mempool, transaction ordering, and
execution costs are what motivate its design choices; a verified EVM backend is
future work, not a result reported here.
\end{abstract}
